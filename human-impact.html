Human Impact of AI-Powered Content Moderation

Behind every “safe platform” there are humans exposed to content no one should see.

Exploring the Human Cost

Content moderators on social media platforms are frequently exposed to extreme material: violence, abuse, self-harm, and death. This exposure is not occasional — it is constant, systematic, and often invisible to the users they protect.

Numerous studies and testimonies reveal that moderators may develop symptoms similar to Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD), anxiety, emotional numbness, and chronic stress.

To move beyond statistics, it is necessary to listen to the people behind the screens.

A Conversation Behind the Screen

To understand the real human cost of moderation, I spoke with a close friend. He moderates several large Discord servers. This is a transcript-style reconstruction.

Content Warning

Most readers skim. Don’t. This is what “keeping communities safe” can look like when a human has to absorb the worst of it.

This format is intentional: it resembles how these conversations really happen — late, quickly, in private channels — while the public sees only the final outcome: “content removed”.

Ethical Responsibility
